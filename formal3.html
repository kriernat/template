Background Information

In order to obtain a bachelor’s of science in packaging degree from Michigan State University, one must take a variety of different courses that involve acquiring both technological and rhetoric literacies. Technical courses that are required include calculus, chemistry, physics, and biology. Then there are the packaging core classes that mostly involve around material science with plastics, glass, metal, paper and paperboard. Also required include fifteen credits of business courses such as accounting, economics, marketing, supply chain management or finance. Then there are the university requirements of social science, humanities and writing. With the diversity of classes that are needed for the degree, a Packaging professional will have a very diverse skillset of literacy when entering the professional world.

Not only is the packaging industry a growing industry but also with MSU ranked as the best Packaging school in the U.S., it makes Packaging one of the most beneficial career paths to explore at MSU. According to Pira International, “the global packaging market is expected to reach $820 billion by 2016” (Butschli). With the industry growth, more jobs will be in demand, which will bode well for future packaging grads around the world as well as MSU packaging grads. According to salary.com, an entry-level packaging engineer in the U.S. is making an average salary of around $59,000 allowing packaging grads to be compensated fairly well for their diverse skillset.
