Communication

In addition to technical literacy, it is also very important for a packaging professional to have literacy in rhetoric content. Rhetoric literacy can include anything in reading, writing, researching and communication. Rhetoric literacy provides the basis for anything someone can obtain that is of technological literacy. If you understand material that was rhetoric there would be no way to obtain technological literacy. Rhetoric literacy plays a huge role in basically any profession that is out there so it is important to have a good understanding of what it is.

Reading can provide a base for basically anything you can do. Obviously, without the ability to read, one would have a very difficult time becoming successful. A packaging professional will need to be able to read anything dealing with technical writing or just common workplace communications such as email. In addition to just reading the content, it is important to comprehend what is going on while reading, which comes from being literate in technological subjects. The ability to read is not only important in anything involved with a job but also in common life situations.

In addition to reading, writing is extremely important in any professional career path but it plays a huge role in packaging as well. Writing in packaging can include anything in communication such as emails, technical write-ups, or even just writing presentations to present. Writing sets the standard for many other things you can do such as the ability to effectively communicate with others or the ability to use technical software such as Microsoft PowerPoint, Excel or Access and effectively get the point the point across.

Writing for workplace communication is an important skill to have in packaging, in order to be able to communicate effectively in a professional manor, it is important to be literate in writing. Business etiquette is not only important in the business world but also in the packaging industry. It is important to have good business etiquette when writing to others you may need to reach. This includes properly addressing emails in a professional way so you come off as a reputable person. This also includes paying your respects with professional “thank you” letters after you ask someone to do something for you. This especially applies for a packaging professional when you may need to speak to a customer or supplier, it is important to send “thank you” letters in order to retain good relations with other companies.

Probably one of the most important qualities to possess in the professional world is the ability to communicate effectively in the workplace. In packaging, it is no different; the ability to understand and be literate in communication is extremely critical in order to be successful. A packaging professional is going to encounter many different people on the job and it is important to understand how to talk to each type of person to get the point across. Packaging professionals will encounter marketing people, finance people, engineers, other packaging professionals and top-level management. In many situations, a packaging professional will be working in cross-functional teams, which means they are working with other people that are not just involved with packaging. So, it is critical a packaging professional know how to effectively communicate in each situation they are involved with.

In addition to dealing with people in their own company, packaging professionals also have to effectively communicate with others outside of the company. This includes customers that you are trying to sell the package to or suppliers that you must buy materials from in order to design and construct the package. Some companies have their own packaging department and others buy straight from packaging companies to obtain their packages for their products. If a packaging professional is working at a packaging company, they will be dealing with customers to sell the package to. In order to convince the customer to purchase the package for their product, it is important for a packaging professional to know how to effectively communicate with a customer. In addition to communicating with customers, packaging professionals also need to know how to effectively communicate with suppliers. In order to design a package, there needs to be raw materials to make the package. So, it is important that a packaging professional know how to effectively communicate with a supplier so they can purchase quality materials at the lowest cost possible.

The last area of communication a packaging professional should understand be literate with is with the Microsoft software package. This includes PowerPoint, Excel, or Access and even Word. PowerPoint and Excel are critical skills that any packaging professional should understand. PowerPoint is a good way for a packaging professional to get a point across and present information to people within a company or directly to a customer. Excel can help to aid with finances and cost of anything a packaging professional may be dealing with. It is important for a packaging professional to be to understand the Excel software and also be able to use it sometimes when trying to estimate costs for a certain package.
